second block
1. abase (v) : lower, degrade, humiliate Usage: Abasing someone in front of others is not a good practice. The criminal conviction served to abase his reputation in the community. 2. **abash (v) **: embarrass Usage: I abashed my teacher when I told him that he's short. He was a little abashed by her regal composure. 3. **abate (v) **: subside or moderate Usage: Rather than leaving immediately, they waited for the storm to abate. He waited for his anger to abate before answering his accuser. 4. **abbreviate (v) **: condense, shorten Usage: Because we were runnig out of time, the lecturer had to abbreviate her spech. The world wide web is often abbreviated to the web or www. 5. **abdicate (v) ** : renounce, give up Usage: The King abdicated when he married a divorcee. Too many parents abdicate their responsibilities and expect the school to teach their children discipline. 6. **aberrant (adj)** : deviate, abnormal Usage: Given the aberrant nature of the data, we came to doubt the validity of the entire experiment. 7. **aberration (n)** : abnormality, mental disorder, deviation from normal. Usage: The problem with many low priced cameras, is color aberration in the final image. An extra finger or toe is an unusual aberration. 8. **abet (v)** : assist, usually in doing something wrong; encourage Usage: she was unwilling to abet him in the swindle he had planned. The Joker helped to abet the bank robbery. 9. **abeyance (v) **: suspended action, pending Usage: The deal was held in abeyance until her arrival. 10.** abhor (v)** : hate, detest, loathe Usage: She abhorred all forms of bigotry. i just abhor questions like this.
